What about adding icons like:

  • ❓️Frequently Asked Questions User:c2main


  • Opposed:
    • Icon fonts are not standardized, and meaning of these different renderings of icons can vary significantly across cultures (most well-known example: Swastika; other examples: "pistol" U+1F52B, "pile of poo" U+1F4A9). The use of textual English language implies knowledge and understanding of English to some degree, while iconography is not limited to this context and thus are much more open to interpretation, which I wouldn't want in a wiki/knowledge base.
    • Furthermore, I find that icons are distracting from the information they supposedly provide, as they add more cruft to parse before getting to the important bits.
    • Lastly, I have yet to find a mainstream physical keyboard that has a key for U+2753, while most keyboards layouts that I know do support immediat input of the ascii characters used in this wiki's markup.
While the question mark is quite common across languages, I think that is one of only few cases where an icon is unambiguous. I don't see what other icons would fit (if any) for the other list items whilst also being unambiguous, non-distracting and universally available for page editors.
- User:Mmeent
